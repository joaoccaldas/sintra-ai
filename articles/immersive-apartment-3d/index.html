Spatial AI · immersive homes · 3D worlds

The apartment is becoming an AI-native interface.

A new class of tools can turn photos, room scans, video or 360 captures into immersive 3D experiences. For apartments, real estate, renovation planning, education and memory capture, this is not just a visual trick. It turns physical space into usable data.

Sintra take

The important shift is not only that a room can look 3D. The shift is that the room becomes data: geometry, surfaces, objects, lighting, movement paths and context. Once a home is represented as a spatial scene, AI can help redesign it, explain it, stage it, sell it, teach inside it, or preserve it as a memory world.

What this tool category does

Capture

Use phone photos, video, 360 camera footage or room scans as raw material.

Reconstruct

Estimate camera motion, depth, surfaces and geometry so flat capture becomes spatial.

Render

Create a navigable 3D scene, Gaussian splat, mesh, point cloud or web viewer.

Transform

Restyle, stage, annotate, teach, sell, preserve or simulate changes inside the space.

Why it matters

Real estate becomes interactive. Buyers can walk through a realistic apartment instead of swiping flat photos. Interior design gets faster. A room becomes the canvas for staging, renovation options and furniture experiments. Education becomes contextual. A kitchen, workshop or apartment can become a spatial lesson instead of a flat page.

What to test

  1. Capture one room from multiple angles in consistent light.
  2. Try both a single-image tool and a video or 360-capture workflow.
  3. Check whether the output is only beautiful or actually navigable.
  4. Look for export formats such as GLB, mesh, point cloud, web viewer or VR viewer.
  5. Test a practical job: staging, renovation planning, memory archive, training scene or sales demo.

Current limits

Flat images can hallucinate depth, door positions, mirrors and object backs. The best outputs still depend on capture discipline: good lighting, overlap and smooth motion. Privacy also matters because uploading your apartment can expose personal possessions, documents and the layout of your home.
