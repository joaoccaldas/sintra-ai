Spatial AI · apartment to immersive 3D

Your apartment becomes a navigable AI memory space.

A new wave of spatial-AI tools can turn room photos, phone video or 360 captures into immersive 3D experiences. The important shift is not the novelty of a prettier virtual tour. It is that a personal space becomes computable: layout, objects, surfaces, lighting, pathways and context.

What changes

From flat photos to spatial interfaces.

Photo-to-3D, Gaussian splatting, neural reconstruction and room-understanding models are converging. The apartment is no longer just captured as a gallery. It can become a scene that AI can navigate, annotate, transform and reason about.

01 · Capture

Record the space

Use phone photos, video, 360 footage or room scans with enough overlap and consistent light.

02 · Reconstruct

Infer depth

The system estimates geometry, camera position and spatial relationships.

03 · Render

Enter the scene

The result becomes a navigable splat, mesh, point cloud, web viewer or VR-like space.

04 · Transform

Change the room

AI can stage, explain, redesign or teach inside the space.

Why this belongs in Sintra AI

Spatial AI extends the AI operating-system idea beyond text. A model that understands a room can become useful for real estate, interior design, insurance, accessibility, training, memory capture and robotics. It is the same Sintra pattern: signal becomes structure, structure becomes decision, decision becomes action.

What to test

  • Capture one room with 20-40 overlapping photos.
  • Compare single-image output against a video or 360 workflow.
  • Check whether the result is actually navigable, not just a pretty render.
  • Look for export formats: GLB, mesh, point cloud, web viewer or embeddable scene.
  • Test one practical use: staging, renovation, sales, training or memory archive.
