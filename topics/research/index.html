Jun 2026Notable

OpenAI Releases LifeSciBench, a 750-Task Life-Science Research Benchmark

OpenAI published LifeSciBench on June 17, 2026, a 750-task benchmark built with 173 PhD-level scientists, grading models on free-response life-science research tasks — including genomic sequence files, chemical structures, and experimental figures — against expert-written rubrics averaging 25 criteria each (19,020 criteria total). The strongest model passed only 36.1% of all tasks and just 28.1% of artifact-heavy tasks, identifying scientific-artifact processing as the primary bottleneck for current AI systems.

Jun 2026Notable

OpenAI publishes "near-autonomous AI chemist" research alongside new lab benchmark

OpenAI released research on June 17 describing a near-autonomous AI system capable of planning and executing multi-step chemistry experiments with limited human intervention. The publication accompanied a new evaluation framework for measuring AI performance on life-sciences research tasks.

Jun 2026Notable

OpenAI Launches Economic Research Exchange for External AI Impact Studies

OpenAI launched the OpenAI Economic Research Exchange on June 9, 2026, a platform inviting selected external researchers to propose privacy-protected, structured collaborations studying AI's economic impacts using OpenAI usage data. The initiative aims to support more rigorous, independent research on labor market and productivity effects of generative AI adoption.

Jun 2026Notable

Sweden, Denmark and Norway formalize Nordic academic AI research alliance

Leaders of national academic AI research centers in Sweden, Denmark, and Norway signed a memorandum of understanding in Oslo on June 8, 2026 to strengthen collaboration and elevate the role of social sciences and humanities in AI research. The partnership aims to create a unified Nordic voice advising policymakers on human-centric, democratic AI governance.

Jun 2026Notable

NeurIPS 2026 Desk-Rejects 178 Papers via AI Detector — False Positive Backlash Erupts

The NeurIPS 2026 Position Paper Track ran 969 submissions through Pangram v3.3.2, desk-rejecting 178 papers and requiring 123 more to prove human authorship by June 15. Researchers — including track chairs whose own papers scored 24–69% AI-probability — reported that heavy human editing still triggered high false-positive scores. The controversy reignited debate about whether AI-detection tools meet the reliability threshold for consequential academic gatekeeping.

Jun 2026Major

Sweden's Arrhenius AI Supercomputer Enters Full Production at NAISS

The Arrhenius AI supercomputer, operated by the National Academic Infrastructure for Supercomputing in Sweden (NAISS) at KTH Royal Institute of Technology, entered full production capacity with 4,096 NVIDIA H100 GPUs delivering 16 exaFLOPS of AI compute. The system is the largest academic AI supercomputer in the Nordic region and is allocated via the Swedish Research Council to universities and research institutions across Sweden.
