Jun 2026Major

NVIDIA RTX Spark: 128 GB Unified-Memory AI PC Unveiled at Computex 2026

NVIDIA announced RTX Spark, a palm-sized AI workstation superchip with 128 GB of unified LPDDR5X memory accessible to both the CPU and GPU at 900 GB/s bandwidth. The device runs 70-billion-parameter LLMs locally at 40+ tokens per second and ships with NVIDIA AI Enterprise pre-installed. Priced at $2,999, it targets enterprise developers who need on-device inference without cloud latency or data-privacy concerns.

Jun 2026Major

NVIDIA Open-Sources Cosmos 3 Omni-Model for Physical AI and Robotics

NVIDIA released Cosmos 3, a multimodal foundation model combining vision, language, and physics simulation for robotics and autonomous systems. Released under the NVIDIA Open Model License, Cosmos 3 can generate photorealistic synthetic training environments, predict object dynamics, and control robot arms with sub-millimetre precision in simulation. It ships with integrations for Isaac Sim, ROS 2, and OpenUSD.

Jun 2026Major

Microsoft Completes $3 B AI Datacenter Expansion in Sweden, Bringing Total to 200k+ Servers

Microsoft announced the completion of its $3 billion AI datacenter expansion programme in Sweden, adding two new halls at the Gävle campus running entirely on renewable hydropower. The expansion brings Microsoft's total Sweden server count to over 200,000, making it the hyperscaler's largest European AI datacenter footprint. The facility will serve Azure AI customers across EMEA with data residency in the EU.

May 2026Major

NVIDIA Confirms Rubin GPU Architecture on Track for H2 2026 Mass Shipment

NVIDIA CEO Jensen Huang confirmed at Computex 2026 that the Rubin GPU architecture — successor to Blackwell — is on schedule for volume customer shipments in the second half of 2026. Rubin introduces a new HBM4 memory subsystem, a redesigned NVLink 6 fabric, and a 3nm TSMC process node. Early Rubin systems are expected to deliver 3–4× the inference throughput of Blackwell B200 per GPU.

May 2026Notable

Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Meta Launch Joint AI Data Center Climate Initiative

Microsoft, Google, Amazon, and Meta announced a joint initiative led by nonprofit Elemental Impact on May 27 to use AI data center buildouts as proving grounds for advanced cooling, energy storage, and low-carbon construction materials. The move comes as AI data centers are projected to consume power equivalent to two-thirds of all US homes. The initiative targets deploying measurable sustainability breakthroughs at scale rather than carbon offset purchases.

May 2026Major

Microsoft Copilot Studio: Computer-Using Agents Now Generally Available

Microsoft's May 2026 Copilot Studio update made computer-using agents generally available in all commercial regions, enabling agents to interact with desktop and web applications exactly as a human would — clicking, typing, and navigating. The update also introduced redesigned Workflows, Work IQ extensibility, and real-time voice. Agent 365, a dedicated IT console for deploying and governing AI agents, reached general availability at $15/user/month.
