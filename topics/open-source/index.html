Together AI Open-Sources OSCAR: 2-Bit KV Cache Quantization for 4x Inference Throughput

Together AI published and open-sourced OSCAR (Offline Spectral Covariance-Aware Rotation) on May 25, 2026, an INT2 KV cache quantization method integrated into SGLang's production serving stack that achieves a 5.3x memory footprint reduction and 4.1x throughput improvement over BF16 baselines. OSCAR addresses the channel-wise outlier problem that previously made 2-bit KV compression impractical by using rotation and clipping to normalize activation distributions before quantization. Sink tokens (first 64) and recent tokens (last 256) are stored in BF16, with all history tokens quantized to INT2.

May 2026Major

TanStack Supply-Chain Attack (CVE-2026-45321, CVSS 9.6) Hits OpenAI, Mistral, EU Commission

Security researchers disclosed that the TeamPCP threat group exploited CVE-2026-45321 (CVSS 9.6) to embed malicious code in the TanStack router ecosystem — a widely used JavaScript routing library. Confirmed victims include OpenAI, Mistral AI, the European Commission, and recruiting platform Mercor, highlighting growing supply-chain vulnerabilities in AI company frontend infrastructure. The incident triggered emergency patching across the JavaScript ecosystem and renewed industry-wide discussion about dependency chain security.

May 2026Notable

Chinese AI Models Now Account for 60% of All Requests on OpenRouter

OpenRouter, the multi-model API aggregator tracking millions of daily LLM calls, reported that Chinese-origin models now handle 60% of total request volume on its platform — up from near-zero two years earlier. The milestone, reported on May 22, is driven by DeepSeek, Qwen, and related open-weight models offering frontier-level performance at a fraction of Western API pricing. The shift reflects a broader trend: cost-sensitive developers and startups are increasingly routing production workloads to Chinese models, intensifying the pressure on OpenAI and Anthropic to defend their pricing.

May 2026Major

DeepSeek Makes 75% V4-Pro API Price Cut Permanent at $0.435/M Tokens

On May 22, 2026, DeepSeek announced its 75% promotional discount on the V4-Pro API would become permanent rather than reverting to list price on June 1. DeepSeek-V4-Pro inference is now permanently priced at $0.435/M input tokens (cache miss), $0.003625/M cache hit, and $0.87/M output — making it approximately 20x cheaper than GPT-5.5 for the same workload. The V4-Pro model features a 1.6T parameter MoE architecture (49B activated) with a 1M token context window.

May 2026Major

Mistral Medium 3.5 — 128B Open-Weight Model With 77.6% SWE-bench

Mistral AI released Medium 3.5, a 128-billion-parameter dense model with a 256,000-token context window, scoring 77.6% on SWE-bench Verified and unifying instruction-following, reasoning, coding, and vision in a single set of weights. The model was released with open weights under a modified MIT license and priced at $1.50/$7.50 per million input/output tokens via API. Mistral simultaneously launched Work Mode in Le Chat — an agentic interface powered by Medium 3.5 that executes multi-step workflows across connected tools in parallel.

Apr 2026Major

DeepSeek V4 — 1.6T-Parameter MoE, 80.6% SWE-bench Verified

DeepSeek released V4-Pro and V4-Flash on April 24, 2026 under MIT license. V4-Pro packs 1.6T total parameters with 49B active via mixture-of-experts, a 1M-token context window, and scored 80.6% on SWE-bench Verified — matching frontier closed-source models. Weights are on Hugging Face; V4-Flash (284B total, 13B active) targets cost-efficient inference.
