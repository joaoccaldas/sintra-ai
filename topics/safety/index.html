May 2026Notable

OpenAI Publishes Frontier Governance Framework Aligned with EU AI Act

OpenAI released its Frontier Governance Framework on May 28, aligning safety practices with California's Transparency in Frontier AI Act and the EU AI Act's Code of Practice for General Purpose AI. The document covers risk assessment across cyber offense, CBRN threats, harmful manipulation, and loss-of-control risks, and supersedes the earlier Preparedness Framework. It includes protocols for model reporting, security incident response, and external expert oversight.

May 2026Landmark

Brazil's Chamber of Deputies Votes on Marco Legal da IA — AI Law Clears Final Legislative Hurdle

Brazil's Chamber of Deputies held the scheduled plenary vote on May 27, 2026 on Bill 2338/2023 — the Marco Legal da Inteligencia Artificial — completing the bill's legislative journey after the Federal Senate unanimously approved it in December 2024. The law adopts a risk-tiered framework modelled on the EU AI Act, classifying AI applications from high-risk (healthcare, credit scoring, biometric surveillance, public services) to low-risk, with fines of up to R$50 million per infraction and primary enforcement by the ANPD. Brazil is positioned to become the first major Latin American economy to enact comprehensive national AI legislation, pending presidential sanction.

May 2026Landmark

Brazil's Chamber of Deputies Passes AI Law 2338 — First Comprehensive AI Legislation in Latin America

Brazil's Chamber of Deputies passed Bill 2338/2023 (Marco Legal da Inteligência Artificial) on May 27, 2026, completing a legislative process that began in the Federal Senate in December 2024. The law establishes a risk-tiered regulatory framework modelled on the EU AI Act, with the ANPD as primary enforcer and fines of up to R$50 million per infraction. High-risk systems in healthcare, credit scoring, biometric surveillance, and public services face strict transparency obligations; the bill now awaits presidential signature.

May 2026Major

DeepMind CEO Hassabis Says AGI Could Arrive by 2029, Society Unprepared

In an Axios interview published May 26, 2026, Google DeepMind CEO Demis Hassabis said he now sees 2029 as a possible AGI arrival date — a year earlier than his previous 2030 estimate — and warned that society, particularly economists, is not taking the implications seriously enough. Speaking after Google I/O, Hassabis described humanity as standing in the 'foothills of the singularity' and said he is actively discussing safety measures with leaders at other top AI labs. He cited current agentic coding systems as a demonstration of the 'soft self-improvement' already underway.

May 2026Notable

Brazil AI Ecosystem Warns New Law May Stifle Startups and Deter Foreign Investment

Following the Chamber vote on AI Bill 2338, Brazilian technology specialists warned that broad liability provisions and tight compliance deadlines could create regulatory uncertainty for Brazil's ~1,400 AI-core startups — a segment that grew over 40% in two years. Only 2.7% of Brazilian AI startup funding comes from foreign investors, far below international norms; analysts drew parallels to the EU AI Act, which contributed to a 15% drop in AI venture capital in Europe in 2024.

May 2026Major

Pentagon Tests OpenAI and Google Models Across Five Theater Commands to Replace Anthropic

Bloomberg reported the Pentagon is evaluating AI models from OpenAI and Google across five military theater commands worldwide, seeking replacements for Anthropic's Claude after Defense Secretary Hegseth designated Anthropic a 'supply chain risk' in March 2026 following its refusal to allow Claude for autonomous weapons or mass civilian surveillance. Anthropic sued the DoD; a federal appeals court panel called the designation 'spectacular overreach' during May 2026 oral arguments. A final ruling is expected in Q3 2026.
