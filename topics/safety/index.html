Jun 2026Major

FERC orders six US grid operators to justify or reform data center connection rules

The Federal Energy Regulatory Commission issued tailored show-cause orders on June 18 to PJM, MISO, SPP, CAISO, ISO-NE and NYISO, giving each 60 days to justify current large-load interconnection tariffs or propose reforms, plus a 30-day deadline to report on generation adequacy. The orders target faster grid access for AI data centers while addressing cost-shifting and co-location rules.

Jun 2026Notable

Macron and Modi headline VivaTech with India as AI country partner

French President Emmanuel Macron and Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi appeared together at VivaTech 2026 on June 18, with India serving as the event's official AI Country Partner for its 10th edition. The event drew over 200,000 visitors and is positioned as a venue for tracking Europe's "AI buildout moving to construction phase."

Jun 2026Notable

EU's 2026 State of the Digital Decade report flags AI adoption gap despite 48% growth

The European Commission's fourth State of the Digital Decade report, published June 17, found AI adoption among EU businesses rose 48% in 2025 but remains under 20%, trailing cloud computing (46.7%) and data analytics (39.9%) adoption. The report warns that structural gaps in compute capacity, skills and scale-up funding could cause the EU to miss its 2030 digital targets.

Jun 2026Major

EU Parliament Casts Final Vote on AI Act Omnibus, Adds Nudifier Ban

The European Parliament voted 423-57-174 on June 16, 2026 to give final approval to the Digital Omnibus package amending the AI Act, postponing high-risk AI obligations to December 2027 (standalone systems) and August 2028 (embedded systems), and delaying watermarking obligations to December 2026. The text adds a new ban on non-consensual AI 'nudification' apps following the earlier Grok-related scandal. Formal Council adoption is expected before the original August 2, 2026 deadline.

Jun 2026Landmark

US Export-Control Directive Forces Anthropic to Suspend Fable 5 and Mythos 5 Access for Foreign Nationals

Anthropic said it received a US government directive on June 12, 2026 at 5:21pm ET ordering it to suspend all access to Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5 for foreign nationals worldwide — including Anthropic's own foreign employees — citing export-control concerns after the government learned of a method to 'jailbreak' Fable 5. Anthropic stated it believes the directive reflects a misunderstanding and is working with the government to restore access as quickly as possible, while partners including AWS began revoking access to comply.

Jun 2026Notable

AI Lab Leaders to Join G7 Leaders' Summit in Évian-les-Bains

Bloomberg reported on June 12, 2026 that executives from Anthropic, OpenAI, Google DeepMind, and Mistral AI — including Dario Amodei, Sam Altman, Demis Hassabis, and Arthur Mensch — are expected to attend the G7 Leaders' Summit in Évian-les-Bains, France, running June 15–17, 2026. France is crafting an agenda that places AI alongside broader economic and security topics for the gathering of G7 heads of state.
