The prompt

Create a brand voice and tone guide for my company. Company: [company name and what it does] Target audience: [who your customers are] Brand personality in 3 words: [e.g., bold, human, precise] Competitors to sound different from: [name 1-2 competitors] Include: 1. Voice pillars (3-4 core characteristics with a definition of each) 2. Tone spectrum — how voice shifts across contexts (e.g., social post vs. error message vs. sales email) 3. Do/don't word list (10 words we use, 10 we avoid) 4. 3 before/after examples rewriting generic copy in our voice 5. One-paragraph 'voice test' to check any new content
Open in Claude

Sample output

**Brand Voice Guide — Volta (B2B SaaS for energy teams)** **Voice Pillars:** 1. **Precise** — We say exactly what we mean. No vague claims, no jargon for its own sake. 2. **Grounded** — We speak like a smart colleague, not a pitch deck. Real examples over abstractions. 3. **Understated confident** — We don't sell hard. We show, let the numbers speak, and trust our audience to get it. **Tone Spectrum:** - Product UI: Direct, minimal (verb + object — 'Add meter', not 'Start adding your meter now') - Blog: Conversational with data — short paragraphs, specific numbers - Error messages: Honest + helpful ('Your file format isn't supported. Try CSV or XLSX.') **Do say:** specific, measurable, because, exactly, trade-off **Don't say:** revolutionary, seamless, world-class, leverage, synergize
