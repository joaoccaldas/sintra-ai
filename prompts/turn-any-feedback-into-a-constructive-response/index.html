The prompt

Help me reframe this feedback I received so I can respond to it professionally and constructively. Feedback I received: [paste the feedback] Context: [who gave it and in what setting — e.g., performance review, Slack message, client email] Give me: 1. What the person likely intended to communicate 2. The valid point inside the feedback (even if it felt harsh) 3. A draft response that acknowledges it without being defensive
Open in Claude

Sample output

**Feedback received:** 'Your presentation was a bit all over the place. It was hard to follow your argument.' **What they likely meant:** The structure wasn't clear enough for the audience to track the logic from slide to slide. **The valid point:** The narrative thread was probably missing — each slide made sense individually but the through-line was unclear. **Draft response:** Thanks for the honest feedback — I appreciate it. I think you're right that the structure could have been clearer. I'll work on building a stronger narrative thread next time, probably by leading with the conclusion and working backwards. Happy to talk through what would have helped.
