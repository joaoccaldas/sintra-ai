Write Branching NPC Dialogue

Three fully-written branching dialogue paths with distinct outcomes for different player approaches.

⬡
Recommended modelClaude Sonnet 4.6

Game logic, dialogue systems, and level design

What you need to fill in

[NPC name][personality description][game setting][player goal in this interaction]

Tools used

ClaudeTwineInkUnity

The prompt

Write branching dialogue for an NPC named [NPC name] who is [personality description] in a [game setting]. The player needs to [player goal in this interaction]. Create 3 conversation paths: friendly approach, aggressive approach, and clever/persuasion approach. Each path should have 3 exchanges and end with a different outcome.
Open in Claude

Sample output

**NPC:** Maren — tired city guard, underpaid, secretly sympathetic **PATH A — Friendly** Player: "Rough shift?" Maren: "You have no idea. Third double this week. What do you need?" Player: "Just trying to get into the merchants' quarter." Maren: *sighs* "Gate closes at ninth bell. But… I didn't see you walk through just now. Move quick." *Outcome: Passage granted, +5 reputation with City Guard faction* **PATH B — Aggressive** Player: "Get out of my way." Maren: "Excuse me? You want to try that again?" *Outcome: Hostile, -10 City Guard reputation, forced combat or retreat* **PATH C — Persuasion** Player: "I heard the captain skims the gate fees. Must be frustrating doing the work and seeing none of it." Maren: *quietly* "Who told you that?" *pause* "Fine. But you didn't hear this from me." *Outcome: Access + quest hook: Expose corrupt captain*
