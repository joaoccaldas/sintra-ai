Identify the top 5 tax optimisation strategies available to me, explain the mechanism of each deduction or deferral, estimate the potential savings, flag any risks or thresholds I need to stay under, and give me a prioritised action checklist for this tax year. **Top 5 Tax Strategies for Your Profile** **1. Max Solo 401(k) — estimated savings: $14,280** As a self-employed person you can contribute up to $66,000 (2024). The employer contribution side (25% of net self-employment income) is deductible on Schedule 1. *IRC §401(a).* Action: open a Solo 401(k) by Dec 31; fund by tax deadline. **2. Home Office Deduction (Simplified Method) — ~$1,500** $5/sq ft × up to 300 sq ft. No depreciation recapture risk. *IRC §280A.* Action: measure dedicated workspace, document with photos. **3. SEP-IRA vs Solo 401(k) comparison** SEP-IRA simpler to open, but Solo 401(k) allows employee deferrals — better if income < $200k. **4. Qualified Business Income (QBI) Deduction — up to 20% of net income** Available for pass-through entities below $182,050 (single). *IRC §199A.* Risk: phase-out applies to specified service trades. **5. Tax-Loss Harvesting on taxable portfolio** Realise ~$8k in unrealised losses to offset gains. Beware 30-day wash-sale rule. *IRC §1091.* **Action checklist:** - [ ] Open Solo 401(k) before Dec 31 - [ ] Calculate exact home office sq footage - [ ] Review taxable account for harvesting candidates - [ ] Confirm QBI eligibility with your business type
