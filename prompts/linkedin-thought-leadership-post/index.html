The prompt

Write a high-performing LinkedIn thought leadership post. My perspective or argument: [the contrarian or insightful take you want to share] My relevant experience: [what gives you credibility to say this] Audience: [who follows you — industry, seniority] Optional: a recent event or data point that makes this timely Format rules: - First line must stand alone as a hook (no preamble) - Short paragraphs (1-3 lines) - No bullet points until the last third if at all - End with a question that invites comments - 200-300 words
Open in Claude

Sample output

Most AI product launches are just feature dumps with a press release. I've reviewed 40+ AI product launches in the past year as part of a VC due diligence process. The ones that converted were almost never the most technically impressive. They had three things in common: They led with a before/after story — not a capability list. They named a specific pain and then showed evidence it was gone. They had a number that was easy to remember. Not '47.3% improvement'. Just 'half the time.' They made the first action stupidly easy. Not 'sign up', not 'book a demo'. 'Paste your first document.' The launches that flopped had one thing in common: they explained what the product does. The successful ones explained what your life is like after you use it. What's the most effective AI product announcement you've seen recently — and why did it work?
