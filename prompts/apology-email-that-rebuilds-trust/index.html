Apology Email That Rebuilds Trust

A sincere, specific apology email that takes accountability and offers a concrete remedy.

⬡
Recommended modelClaude Sonnet 4.6

Best-in-class writing with deep contextual nuance

What you need to fill in

[what happened][who was affected][root cause if known][what you've fixed][what you're offering]

Tools used

ClaudeChatGPT

The prompt

Write a genuine apology email that rebuilds trust after a service failure or mistake. What happened: [describe the issue or failure] Who was affected: [customers, employees, partners?] Root cause (if known): [what actually caused it] What you've fixed or are fixing: [concrete actions taken] What you're offering as remedy: [credit, refund, extra support, etc.] Rules: - No passive voice ('mistakes were made' → 'we made a mistake') - No 'we apologize for any inconvenience' (cliché) - Acknowledge the impact, not just the event - Be specific about what changed - Short sentences. No PR spin.
Open in Claude

Sample output

**Subject: We let you down last Tuesday — here's what happened and what we changed** On May 20th, our platform was down for 4 hours and 17 minutes. During that window, you couldn't access your data or run reports. That's unacceptable. We know some of you had client meetings. Some of you had board reports due. **What caused it:** A database migration we ran at 2am UTC hit an edge case we hadn't tested. Our rollback took longer than it should have because our runbook for this scenario was out of date. **What we've fixed:** - Updated rollback runbook and tested it twice - Added monitoring for the edge case that triggered the failure - Moving all infrastructure changes to a 72-hour review window **What we're giving you:** 15 days of credit added to your account automatically. If you were directly affected and want to talk through what happened, reply and I'll call you. — [Name], CEO
