Instant Habit Tracker Design

A minimal, sustainable habit tracking system you can start using today.

⬡
Recommended modelClaude Haiku 4.5

Instant responses for simple, high-frequency tasks

What you need to fill in

[list 3-5 habits][your schedule][preferred format]

Tools used

ClaudeChatGPT

The prompt

Help me design a simple habit tracking system I can start today. Habits I want to track: [list 3-5 habits] My schedule: [morning person or evening? how many minutes can I commit to tracking?] Preferred format: [paper notebook / spreadsheet / phone app / Notion] Give me: 1. A minimal daily check-in format (should take under 2 minutes) 2. A weekly review question I can ask myself on Sundays 3. The single most common reason people quit habit trackers — and how to avoid it 4. A 30-day milestone I should aim for before changing anything
Open in Claude

Sample output

**Habits:** Exercise 20 min, read 10 pages, no phone after 9pm **Daily check-in (Notion, 90 seconds):** ``` Date: __ Exercise: ☐ Done ☐ Skip — reason: __ Reading: ☐ Done ☐ Skip — reason: __ No phone: ☐ Done ☐ Partial ☐ Failed One word for today: __ ``` **Weekly review question (Sunday):** 'Which habit had the most skips this week, and was it a circumstance or a decision?' **Why people quit:** They track too many things. Start with your 3, add nothing for 30 days. **30-day milestone:** Aim for 20/30 days completed on each habit (67%) — not perfection. At day 30, ask: which habit costs the most friction to start? That's the one to improve.
