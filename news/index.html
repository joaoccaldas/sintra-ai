UK CMA

UK CMA Orders Google to Let Publishers Opt Out of AI Search Summaries — World-First Ruling

The UK Competition and Markets Authority announced on June 3 that it is ordering Google to give UK publishers the ability to opt out of having their content used in AI Overviews and AI Mode search results, without losing visibility in standard Google Search — a world-first regulatory intervention. Google is also barred from penalising the rankings of publishers that opt out, must provide separate controls for AI training and grounding use of content, and must publish clear attribution links. Google has nine months to implement the changes and must submit regular compliance reports; the order follows the CMA's October 2025 designation of Google as holding 'strategic market status' in UK search.

Why it mattersThe CMA's opt-out model could become the global template for publisher-AI platform relationships — if Google complies, every major jurisdiction from the EU to Australia will likely adopt similar 'strategic market status' opt-out mechanisms for AI search.

Try itIf you manage a content-heavy site with UK traffic, monitor Google's compliance roadmap: the nine-month deadline (March 2027) means you can begin scoping opt-out preferences now before the tooling is live.
