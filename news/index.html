Google Releases DiffusionGemma, a 26B Open-Weight Text-Diffusion Model 4x Faster Than Gemma 4

Google DeepMind released DiffusionGemma, a 26B-parameter Mixture-of-Experts model (3.8B active, built on the Gemma 4 backbone) that generates text via diffusion instead of autoregressive decoding, producing whole blocks of text in parallel. It delivers up to 4x faster generation — over 700 tokens/sec on an RTX 5090 and 1,000+ tokens/sec on a single H100 — but scores lower than standard Gemma 4 on MMLU and coding benchmarks, and Google recommends it for low-latency single-user workloads rather than quality-critical production use.

Why it mattersText-diffusion generation is a structurally different decoding approach from the autoregressive models that dominate today's LLMs, and a 4x speed gain — even with a quality tradeoff — opens new local and on-device use cases.

Try itRun DiffusionGemma locally via Hugging Face Transformers or vLLM on an RTX or DGX Spark machine for latency-sensitive prototyping, but keep Gemma 4 for tasks where benchmark accuracy matters.
