South Korea

South Korea Unveils an $880 Billion, 10-Year AI, Chip and Robotics Plan

President Lee Jae-myung announced a national plan to invest at least $880 billion (about 1,350 trillion won) over the next decade in semiconductors, AI data centers, and robotics — his 'Three Mega Projects.' Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix are expected to put in roughly 800 trillion won (~$518 billion) to build new chip-production hubs in the country's southwest, while groups including SK, GS, and Naver back large-scale AI data-center construction with about 550 trillion won ($356 billion).

Why it mattersIt's one of the largest state-backed AI-hardware commitments anywhere — a bet that the next phase of the AI race is won in chips, memory (HBM), and data centers, not just models.

Try itIf you depend on GPU/HBM supply or Asian data-center capacity, track where Korea's southwest chip hubs and data centers land — it reshapes the 2027–2030 compute map.

Claude Sonnet 5 Beats Sonnet 4.6 Across Benchmarks and Narrows the Gap to Opus 4.8

Coverage from The New Stack and others noted Sonnet 5 improves on Sonnet 4.6 on every published benchmark — roughly 63.2% on SWE-bench Pro (vs 58.1%), 76.1% on Terminal-bench (vs 55.4%), and 81.2% on OSWorld-Verified — and on the GDPval-AA v2 knowledge-work eval it scored 1,618, edging Opus 4.8's 1,615. Anthropic's system card also reports lower hallucination and sycophancy, better refusal of malicious requests, and stronger resistance to prompt injection in agentic contexts.

Why it mattersA mid-tier model matching Opus on a knowledge-work eval while cutting hallucination and prompt-injection risk is exactly the reliability profile enterprises need before trusting agents with real workflows.

Try itRe-run your own agentic eval or a known-tricky task set on Sonnet 5 and watch specifically for reduced hallucination and better tool-use recovery, not just headline benchmark scores.

OpenAI and Broadcom Unveil 'Jalapeño,' OpenAI's First Custom Inference Chip

OpenAI and Broadcom unveiled Jalapeño, OpenAI's first custom AI accelerator, a blank-slate design built specifically for LLM inference rather than adapted from general-purpose AI hardware. Co-developed from initial design to tape-out in roughly nine months, OpenAI says early results show significantly better performance-per-watt than current state-of-the-art alternatives, with initial deployment targeted by the end of 2026. Why it mattersA purpose-built inference ASIC is OpenAI's clearest move yet to control its own compute stack and cut dependence on Nvidia, and a nine-month design-to-tape-out cycle signals how fast custom silicon can now ship when the buyer co-designs it.

Try itTrack whether Jalapeño's end-of-2026 deployment translates into lower OpenAI API inference pricing — custom inference silicon is ultimately a bet on cost-per-token.

In a June 10 letter to the US Senate Banking Committee made public on June 24, Anthropic accused operators affiliated with Alibaba's Qwen lab of running roughly 28.8 million model exchanges via about 25,000 fraudulent accounts between April 22 and June 5 to extract Claude's software-engineering and agentic-reasoning capabilities.
