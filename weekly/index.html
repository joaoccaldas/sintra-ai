European Commissionmajor

European Commission Picks Domyn-Led EUROPA Consortium for Frontier AI Grand Challenge

The European Commission selected the EUROPA consortium, led by Italian company Domyn, as winner of its Frontier AI Grand Challenge launched in February 2026. EUROPA will build an open-source frontier AI model covering all 24 official EU languages with computational capacity equivalent to at least 400 billion parameters, using efficient Mixture-of-Experts architectures, and will receive up to 2.5% of EuroHPC's total AI-optimized supercomputing capacity for one year.

Why it mattersThis is the EU's most concrete step yet toward a sovereign, EU-language-native frontier model trained on European public supercomputing infrastructure rather than US or Chinese compute.

OpenAInotable

OpenAI Releases LifeSciBench, a 750-Task Life-Science Research Benchmark

OpenAI published LifeSciBench on June 17, 2026, a 750-task benchmark built with 173 PhD-level scientists, grading models on free-response life-science research tasks — including genomic sequence files, chemical structures, and experimental figures — against expert-written rubrics averaging 25 criteria each (19,020 criteria total). The strongest model passed only 36.1% of all tasks and just 28.1% of artifact-heavy tasks, identifying scientific-artifact processing as the primary bottleneck for current AI systems.

Why it mattersA sub-40% pass rate from the strongest model on real-world, artifact-heavy science tasks is a useful corrective against benchmark scores on cleaner multiple-choice biology tests.

Claude Fable 5 — Anthropic's Most Capable Model Yet, 95% on SWE-bench Verified

Anthropic released Claude Fable 5 on June 9, 2026, alongside the restricted Claude Mythos 5, describing it as its most capable generally-available model to date.
