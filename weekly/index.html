OpenAI Rolls Out 'Dreaming V3' Hierarchical Memory Architecture to ChatGPT

OpenAI began deploying Dreaming V3, a hierarchical background-synthesis memory system, to ChatGPT Plus and Pro users in the US on June 4, 2026. Why it mattersPersistent, self-updating memory moves ChatGPT closer to a continuous personal assistant; the 5× efficiency gain signals that near-zero-cost memory at scale is now feasible.

EU Appoints 60-Expert Scientific Panel to Enforce AI Act Ahead of August Deadline

The European Commission formally appointed a 60-member Scientific Panel and a 174-member Advisory Forum on June 2, 2026 to support enforcement of the EU AI Act. The Scientific Panel — composed of leading independent experts in frontier AI, engineering, and technical auditing — will focus on GPAI model classification, systemic-risk assessment, evaluation methodologies, and cross-border market surveillance. Most AI Act rules apply from August 2, 2026.

Why it mattersWith full AI Act enforcement nine weeks away, appointing the Scientific Panel gives the EU AI Office technical capacity to evaluate frontier models — a critical missing piece for credible GPAI oversight.

European Commissionmajor

EU Proposes Cloud and AI Development Act (CADA) — Triple EU Datacenter Capacity, Cloud Sovereignty Framework

The European Commission presented the Cloud and AI Development Act (CADA), targeting tripling EU datacenter capacity within 5–7 years to fully meet EU enterprise demand by 2035. CADA introduces a 4-tier EU Cloud Sovereignty Framework classifying cloud services by supply chain control, data residency, and infrastructure location. Commission President von der Leyen stated: 'We want to be sure nobody has a kill switch.' The package also includes Chips Act 2.0 and an Open Source Strategy.

Why it mattersTripling EU datacenter capacity is a structural industrial policy that would reduce European AI infrastructure dependency on US hyperscalers — the most consequential EU tech sovereignty move since GDPR.

Analysts expect a committee vote before the August recess, but two unresolved issues — copyright liability for AI training data and the definition of high-risk systems — remain contentious. With October 2026 elections approaching, the legislative window is shrinking rapidly.

Why it mattersIf passed, Brazil's AI Act would create a risk-tiered regulatory framework covering 215 million people and the largest AI market in Latin America.

EU Cloud and AI Development Act Text Released, Mandates Compute Reporting for Hyperscalers

The European Commission released the provisional text of the EU Cloud and AI Development Act in early June 2026, requiring hyperscalers operating in the EU to report AI compute capacity to the AI Office quarterly, share excess compute with European academic institutions at below-market rates, and submit AI-training energy consumption data. The Act pairs with the AI Act's GPAI obligations ahead of the August 2026 enforcement date.

Why it mattersMandatory compute reporting places a new compliance burden on AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud in Europe — and the academic compute-sharing clause could meaningfully reduce training costs for European researchers.
